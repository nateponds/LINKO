Executive Summary
This business plan discusses the issues encountered by small and medium sized
businesses regarding supply chain management. Factors contributing to the
bottlenecking of supply chain logistics and its efficiency include manual and traditional
methods of supply management, decentralized communication mediums of instruction
and notification, and the lack of visibility in monitoring ongoing shipment routes across
the supply chain.
The planning development exists solely to support and improve the business
process flow of small and medium sized business owners. Despite small purchases of
bulk quantities of supplies from their designated warehouses due to limited capital and
inconsistent cashflow of a small business, the frequency of such incoming deliveries in
short periods of time can become an inconvenience in dealing with using traditional
methods and manual tools of supply chain management. In order to support the growth
and efficiency of small businesses, we aim to develop Linko, a logistics supply chain
management application specifically designed to support small to medium enterprises
and businesses in tracking their supply chain ecosystem, find available suppliers, and
improve management

Description of the Business
Linko is a specialized Supply Chain Management (SCM) platform engineered to
bridge the widening digital divide within the Philippine retail sector. While global
e-commerce leaders utilize high-tier logistics automation, the local market—specifically
small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs), franchise stores, and rural
retailers—remains heavily reliant on antiquated, manual systems. Currently, the industry
standard for these businesses involves physical logbooks for inventory tracking and
fragmented messaging apps (e.g., Viber, Messenger) for supplier coordination. This
lack of a centralized, professional medium results in operational "blind spots," leading to
wasted labor, inefficient financial management, and a significant barrier to scaling in a
digital-first economy.
The Linko platform transforms these logistical challenges into opportunities for
operational improvement. By providing a unified mobile interface, Linko offers real-time
GPS tracking for incoming shipments, allowing business owners to monitor their supply
chain with precision. Complementing this is an automated inventory management
system designed to eliminate the human errors inherent in manual logging. By digitizing
these core processes, Linko allows entrepreneurs to reclaim the time previously lost to
administrative coordination, enabling a shift in focus toward business growth and
customer engagement.
Optimizing Supply Chain Transparency
Linko is dedicated to eliminating the "blind spots" that characterize the traditional
Philippine delivery process. By integrating a real-time interface for shipment routes, the
13

platform ensures that business owners no longer face uncertainty regarding incoming
stock. This transparency allows retailers to monitor exactly when shipments leave the
warehouse and track their progress toward the store, effectively mitigating the risks
associated with unmonitored transit. This focused approach to visibility transforms
supply chain management from a reactive manual task into a proactive, data-driven
operational strategy.
Democratizing Professional Logistics Tools
A core objective of Linko is to provide local entrepreneurs with access to the
same sophisticated digital infrastructure utilized by global enterprises. By replacing
fragmented communication mediums like text messages and social media with a
centralized platform, Linko establishes a professional ecosystem tailored for the specific
needs of SMEs and sari-sari stores. The inclusion of features such as a "Supplier
Finder" creates a marketplace within the app, allowing business owners to discover and
connect with available suppliers with ease. This democratized access to technology
levels the playing field, ensuring that even rural retailers have the necessary tools to
optimize their operations and scale effectively.
Aspiring for Market Leadership in the SME Sector
Linko aims to achieve a dominant position within the local B2B logistics space by

The Product or Service
Linko is a mobile application that centralizes and automates supply chain
management for small businesses. It brings together four core features into a single,
easy-to-use platform:
Centralized Communication and Tracking
The Centralized Communication Hub functions as a professional alternative to
the fragmented use of personal social media and messaging applications. This feature
allows business owners and suppliers to coordinate orders, share status updates, and
manage the entire supply chain within a unified and secure environment.
Complementing this is Real-Time Delivery Tracking, which provides a live map interface
showing the precise location and current status of incoming shipments. This
transparency ensures that business owners can actively monitor transit progress,
effectively eliminating the "blind period" that often occurs once a delivery leaves the
warehouse.
Market Integration and Inventory Automation
To facilitate growth and procurement, the Supplier Finder serves as an integrated
marketplace within the app where business owners can discover and connect with new
distributors. Suppliers utilize this feature to list their product availability, creating a direct
bridge between local demand and available stock. Furthermore, Automated Inventory
Logging removes the historical reliance on traditional paper logbooks and manual
16

spreadsheets by instantly updating inventory records upon the receipt and confirmation
of a shipment. This automation not only reduces the potential for manual error but also
allows entrepreneurs to focus their resources on business development rather than
administrative maintenance.
Strategic Procurement Insights
A newly integrated feature, Strategic Procurement Insights, provides owners with
simplified data visualizations of their purchasing history and inventory turnover. This
addition addresses the specific challenges of limited capital and inconsistent cash flow
by helping business owners analyze the frequency of their small-bulk deliveries. By
providing a clear overview of supply routes and turnover rates, Linko turns logistical
data into actionable opportunities for improvement, enabling retailers to make more
informed decisions regarding their inventory investments.

Analysis of the Competition
While the logistics industry is dominated by large-scale enterprise solutions
tailored for multinational corporations, the local SME sector currently operates within a
fragmented landscape of traditional methods and non-specialized tools. Linko’s primary
competition is not found in established software rivals, but rather in the entrenched
"status quo" of manual operations that have dictated local commerce for decades. By
identifying the limitations of these existing workarounds, Linko positions itself as the
necessary evolution for businesses looking to overcome the inefficiencies of traditional
logistics.
Direct and Indirect Competitors
The most significant indirect competitor to Linko is the widespread reliance on
traditional methods, often referred to locally as the "blue logbook" system. While these
manual tools are zero-cost and require no technical literacy, they are fundamentally
inefficient and highly susceptible to human error. Paper-based systems offer no
capability for real-time tracking or proactive operational planning, leaving business
owners in a reactive state whenever delivery delays occur. Furthermore, many
entrepreneurs currently rely on a disorganized combination of consumer-grade
messaging apps such as Viber, Messenger, and WhatsApp to coordinate with their
suppliers. Although these platforms are familiar and accessible, they lack the
organizational structure required for professional supply chain management, leading to
"information silos" where critical shipment data is frequently lost in casual chat histories.
21

Existing general inventory applications represent a more direct form of
competition; however, these platforms typically focus on internal stock management and
fail to address the critical "in-transit" visibility gap. Most of these apps are designed for
warehouse environments rather than the dynamic reality of a retailer waiting for an
incoming shipment from a distant supplier. By failing to integrate real-time GPS data
with inventory logging, these applications only solve half of the logistical puzzle, leaving
a significant opening for a more comprehensive, specialized solution like Linko.

Linko’s Competitive Edge
Linko’s edge is defined by its ability to provide a unified ecosystem that
transforms fragmented data into a streamlined operational advantage. Unlike generic

entry for digital transformation. This unique combination of high-tier tracking technology

entrepreneurs who find traditional enterprise software too complex and manual methods
